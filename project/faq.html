Frequently Asked Questions

Getting Started

Q: What is real estate investing?

Real estate investing involves purchasing, owning, managing, renting and/or selling property for profit. This can include residential properties (single-family homes, multi-family units), commercial properties (office buildings, retail spaces) or land.

Q: How much money do I need to start investing in real estate?

The required capital varies based on your investment strategy:

  • REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts): As little as $100
  • House Hacking: 3.5% - 20% down payment
  • Traditional Investment Property: Typically 20 - 25% down payment
  • Wholesale Real Estate: Minimal upfront capital, but requires time & market knowledge
  • Real Estate Crowdfunding: Often $500 - $5,000 minimum investment

Investment Strategies

Q: What are the main strategies for making money in real estate?
  1. Appreciation
    • Buy and hold property for long-term value increase
    • Make improvements to increase property value
    • Benefit from market growth
  2. Cash Flow
    • Rental income from tenants
    • Regular monthly income after expenses
    • Focus on positive cash flow properties
  3. Fix and Flip
    • Purchase undervalued properties
    • Renovate to increase value
    • Sell for profit
  4. BRRRR Method
    • Buy
    • Rehab
    • Rent
    • Refinance
    • Repeat
Q: What is house hacking and how does it work?

House hacking involves:

  • Purchasing a multi-unit property
  • Living in one unit
  • Renting out the other units
  • Using rental income to cover mortgage and expenses
  • Potentially living for free or reduced cost
  • Learning property management firsthand

Financial Considerations

Q: How do I calculate potential returns on a real estate investment?

Key metrics include:

  • Cap Rate = (Net Operating Income / Property Value) × 100
  • Cash-on-Cash Return = (Annual Cash Flow / Total Cash Invested) × 100
  • ROI = ((Gain - Cost) / Cost) × 100
  • Net Operating Income (NOI) = Gross Income - Operating Expenses

  3. Financial Risks
